Project
Year2022–23
TypeResidential Renovation — Design & Documentation
LocationPimlico, London, United Kingdom
ThemeLight · Material · Spatial Identity

Since the property is a basement flat, it was important to carefully consider shadow, colour, and lighting to make each room feel distinct and purposeful. The challenge of a below-ground dwelling is the absence of natural light from above — every spatial decision had to compensate for this, creating a sense of warmth, depth, and identity through material choice and artificial light.

As the space evolved, we produced architectural drawings initially for our own DIY efforts and later for use by contractors. These drawings proved essential, especially since none of the contractors were native English speakers — the clarity of the visuals helped minimise misunderstandings and ensured smoother construction throughout the project.

The project became an exercise in restraint and precision: working within fixed structural constraints, each room was given a distinct identity through considered choices of finish, fixture, and furniture. The drawings served not only as construction documents but as a design tool — a way of testing spatial decisions before committing to them physically.
