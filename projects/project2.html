Cultivating Creative Resilience

Project
Year2023–24
DiplomaYear 4 — Diploma 9 Free House
LocationHarringay Warehouse District, Haringey, London, UK
ThemeLive/Work · Creative Community · Adaptive Reuse · Brewery

London is in the midst of a housing crisis characterised by high rents, limited space, and unaffordable new builds. Many find themselves priced out and struggling to find a place to call home. Located in the borough of Haringey is the Harringay Warehouse District — these warehouses, transformed into living and working spaces, transcend conventional living. As more warehouses became inhabited, like-minded creative individuals were drawn to the sites, fostering a community of like-minded people.

Concealed from the general public and the watchful eyes of the council, the discretion of the needs of a determined artistic crowd alongside their strong bond to their landlords saw a unique evolution — a gradual establishment of a new form of living. Despite the community's growth, official building legislation was not designed to support unconventional typologies, casting doubt on the warehouse district's sustainability.

Large communal areas allow for exhibitions, performances, and workshops, providing opportunities to exhibit work and engage with the broader community. Private bedrooms — larger than standard and free to customisation — are adjacent to large communal spaces occupying half the warehouse, providing areas where work and leisure coexist. Today the site is home to over 1,000 visionaries: architects, tattoo artists, theatre performers, rave organisers, and a variety of less conventional creatives. Rollover contracts ensure residents are never tied to lengthy fixed tenancies, while the space encourages a range of startups and grassroots businesses.

One intervention involves the introduction of a brewery and nightclub. Leveraging the productive potential of these spaces generates revenue and subsidises housing costs. Beer is produced and sold on site at a small scale, carried out by the live-in tenants, generating a stable passive income that leaves sufficient time for their own creative endeavours. The process from creation to production makes full use of the existing skills available on site.
